AN EVENING OF VIETNAMESE MUSIC & POETRY

+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+
"Tiptoeing to Deliver Unfinished Words"

With Vietnamese Poet-in-Exile Du Tu Le and Supporting Performing Artists
+-+-+-+- +-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+-+



Saturday, April 16 2005
8:00- 10:00pm
HARVARD SQUARE
Winthrop House, JCR

FREE for Harvard students


$5 students, $10 general admission
(available at the door)

Come experience the works of renowned Vietnamese poet Du Tu Le. Listen to Vietnamese poetry-chants, and beautiful guitar and vocal performances of popular songs derived from Du Tu Le’s poetry.

All performances will have English translations.

Du Tu Le was born in 1942 in northern Vietnam.  In 1954, the young Du Tu Le moved to the South in the great migration following the split of the country. He served as a former officer in the Army of the Republic of Vietnam (ARVN). In 1973, his poetry received the National Literature Award in South Vietnam.  Now living in the U.S., Du Tu Le is one of the most prolific poets living in exile, with thirty-six works of poetry and prose. His poetry is read in universities throughout North America and Europe. He has been published in the New York Times, the Los Angeles Times, and World Poetry: An Anthology of Verses from Antiquity to Our Time (1998).
